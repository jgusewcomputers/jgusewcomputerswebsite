Services

Email cleanup and digital organisation.

A cluttered inbox, too many old accounts, or no clear system for where things live are common problems. We help you build a clear picture and a simpler setup — without asking for your password or logging into anything.

What we help with

  • Email account cleanup — working out what to keep, what to archive, what to unsubscribe from, and how to set up folders or labels that make sense for how you actually use email.
  • Account separation — if personal and business email are mixed together, or old accounts are still receiving important messages, we help you map what exists and plan a cleaner arrangement.
  • Retiring old accounts safely — closing or migrating away from an old email address without losing access to accounts that depend on it. This requires careful planning — we walk through it step by step.
  • Digital organisation — a simple structure for where files live: what goes in cloud storage, what stays local, what should be backed up. A clear device map so you know what's where.
  • Safer habits — practical suggestions for reducing inbox noise, avoiding phishing, and keeping accounts more secure without memorising complex rules.

Important: how this works safely

All advice is given by email. We do not log into your accounts, we do not ask for your password, and we do not use remote access software.

You describe the situation — what accounts you have, what the problem is, what you'd like to change — and we reply with clear, step-by-step guidance you can follow yourself. If a step feels unclear, we'll explain it again a different way.

Honest expectations

  • We do not log into or access your email account on your behalf
  • We do not recover deleted emails — that depends on your email provider's settings
  • We do not manage your inbox on an ongoing basis
  • Complex migrations (e.g. moving years of email between providers) take time and planning — we'll be realistic about the effort involved

Describe the email or organisation situation you'd like to sort out and we'll put together a clear plan — step by step, in plain English.
